## Experimental Design

### 1. Evaluating FRESHPROMPT on GPT-3.5 and GPT-4

- **Procedure**:
  - Use the FRESHQA dataset, which consists of 600 questions divided into four categories: never-changing, slow-changing, fast-changing, and false-premise.
  - For each question, apply the FRESHPROMPT method by incorporating search engine results into the prompt. This involves:
    - Querying a search engine (GOOGLE SEARCH) with the question to retrieve relevant information.
    - Extracting text snippets, source, date, title, and highlighted words from the search results.
    - Sorting the retrieved evidences chronologically from oldest to newest.
    - Including few-shot demonstrations at the beginning of the prompt to guide the model in reasoning over the evidences.
  - Use GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 models to generate answers based on the augmented prompts.
  - Evaluate the models on the test set of 500 questions, with 125 questions per category.

- **What to Record**:
  - Raw outputs of the models.
  - Extracted answers from the model outputs.
  - Accuracy of the answers under both RELAXED and STRICT evaluation modes.
  - Hallucination rate, defined as the difference in accuracy between RELAXED and STRICT modes.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Accuracy**: The percentage of questions for which the model's primary answer is correct.
  - **Hallucination Rate**: Calculated as the difference in accuracy between RELAXED and STRICT evaluation modes.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
