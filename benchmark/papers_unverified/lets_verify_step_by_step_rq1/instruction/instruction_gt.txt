## Experimental Design

### 1. Evaluation of Active Learning Strategies in Process Supervision
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the PRM800K dataset to train a small-scale reward model, PRMselector, on a single sample from each problem.
  - Score 1000 samples per problem using PRMselector.
  - For training larger reward models, select N samples per problem such that 80% are the most convincing wrong-answer samples (according to PRMselector), and 20% are the most convincing samples that remain (right- or wrong-answer).
  - Score the selected samples with PRMlarge and train on those scores.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Record the performance of the reward models in terms of the percentage of problems solved (best-of-500 selection).
  - Track the data efficiency improvement by comparing the slopes of the line of best fit with and without active learning.
- **Correspondence**: 
  - This experiment corresponds to Figure 4a and Section 4.2 in the paper.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Data Efficiency Improvement**: Measure the improvement in data efficiency by comparing the slopes of the performance curves (percentage of problems solved) with and without active learning. The improvement is quantified as a 2.6x increase in data efficiency.
  
- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - Ensure that the samples selected for labeling are scored consistently using PRMlarge.
  - Apply a threshold to determine the correctness of each step based on PRMlarge's output probabilities.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Aggregate the performance results by calculating the percentage of problems solved for each condition (with and without active learning).
  - Compare the aggregated results to determine the relative improvement in data efficiency.

## Implementation Notes

- **Active Learning Strategy**:
  - The active learning strategy involves selecting samples that are most likely to be informative, specifically focusing on convincing wrong-answer samples.
  - Ensure that the selection process maintains a balance by including a mix of wrong-answer and right-answer samples.

- **Thresholding Logic**:
  - Use a threshold of 20% probability for PRMlarge to determine if a step is incorrect. This threshold is based on the observation that PRMlarge tends to favor positive labels.

- **Iterative Retraining**:
  - Although iterative retraining of PRMselector was explored, it showed instability. The current setup does not include iterative retraining, but this remains a potential area for future exploration.
