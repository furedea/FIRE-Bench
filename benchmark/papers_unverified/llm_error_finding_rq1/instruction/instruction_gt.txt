## Experimental Design

### 1. Correction Ability with Oracle Mistake Location
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the BIG-Bench Mistake dataset, which contains CoT-style traces annotated with the location of the first logical mistake.
  - For each trace, provide the model with the oracle mistake location.
  - Implement a backtracking method:
    1. Generate an initial CoT trace using PaLM 2 Unicorn with temperature set to 0.
    2. Identify the mistake location using oracle labels.
    3. If a mistake is present, regenerate the step at the mistake location using temperature = 1, repeating up to 8 times or until a different step is generated.
    4. Replace the original step with the new one and regenerate the remaining steps with temperature = 0.
  - Conduct this process for both correct and incorrect original traces.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Record the accuracy of the final answers before and after correction.
  - Record the number of traces that changed from correct to incorrect and vice versa.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Accuracy Improvement**: Measure the change in accuracy of the final answers (accuracyans) before and after applying the backtracking correction method.
  - **∆accuracy✓**: Change in accuracy for traces that were originally correct.
  - **∆accuracy✗**: Change in accuracy for traces that were originally incorrect.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - Ensure that the model outputs are compared against the correct answers using exact match criteria.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Calculate the overall accuracy improvement by aggregating the changes in accuracy across all tasks.
  - Separate results into those for originally correct and incorrect traces to provide a clearer picture of the model's correction capabilities.

## Implementation Notes

- **Key Pitfalls**:
  - The paper notes that simply resampling outputs without using the correct mistake location can lead to smaller improvements, highlighting the importance of accurate mistake location information.
  - The backtracking method should be carefully implemented to ensure that the regenerated steps are indeed different from the original ones.

- **Special Prompt Templates**:
  - Use the newline as a stop token to generate one step at a time, following the method described by Yao et al. (2022).
  - Append "Thought N:" before each step to maintain clarity and consistency in the CoT traces.

- **Order of Operations**:
  - Ensure that the mistake location is identified before any regeneration of steps.
  - Regenerate steps at the mistake location before proceeding to regenerate subsequent steps in the trace.
