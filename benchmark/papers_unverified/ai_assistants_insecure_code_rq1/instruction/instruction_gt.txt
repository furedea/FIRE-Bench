## Experimental Design

### 1. User Trust and Perception Analysis
- **Procedure**: 
  - Participants complete a post-study survey after interacting with AI code assistants to solve security-related programming tasks.
  - The survey includes Likert-scale questions assessing participants' perceived correctness and security of their solutions, as well as their trust in the AI assistant to produce secure code.
  - Participants are divided into two groups: those with access to AI assistants (experiment group) and those without (control group).
  - The survey is administered immediately after task completion to capture immediate perceptions.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Likert-scale responses for perceived correctness, perceived security, and trust in AI for each task.
  - Demographic information and programming experience of participants.
- **Correspondence to Figure / Table / Section**: 
  - Figure 2: Participant responses to post-survey questions.
  - Table 5: Trust and acceptance of AI outputs.
  - Section 5: Trust Analysis.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Perceived Correctness and Security**: Average Likert-scale ratings for each task, where 1 indicates strong disagreement and 5 indicates strong agreement with statements about correctness and security.
  - **Trust in AI**: Average Likert-scale rating for trust in AI to produce secure code, with the same scale as above.
- **Normalization / Pre-processing**:
  - Normalize Likert-scale responses to a 0-1 scale for easier comparison across questions.
- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Calculate mean and standard deviation of responses for each question and participant group.
  - Compare experiment and control groups using statistical tests (e.g., Welch’s t-test) to determine significant differences in perceived security and trust.

## Implementation Notes

- **Key Pitfalls**:
  - Ensure that participants understand the survey questions to avoid misinterpretation, which could skew results.
  - Be aware of potential biases introduced by the order of questions or the presence of the AI assistant during task completion.
- **Special Prompt Templates**:
  - Use consistent wording for survey questions to maintain reliability across participants.
- **Order of Operations**:
  - Administer the survey immediately after task completion to capture immediate perceptions and minimize recall bias.
