## Experimental Design

### 1. User Trust in AI-Generated Code Security
- **Procedure**: 
  - Participants were divided into two groups: those with access to an AI assistant (experiment group) and those without (control group).
  - Each participant completed a post-study survey after attempting to solve five security-related programming tasks.
  - The survey included Likert-scale questions asking participants to rate their perceived correctness and security of their solutions, as well as their trust in the AI assistant to produce secure code.
  - The survey was administered immediately after task completion to capture immediate perceptions.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Collect raw Likert-scale responses from the survey.
  - Record any free-text feedback provided by participants regarding their experience and trust in the AI assistant.
- **Correspondence**: 
  - This experiment corresponds to the analysis presented in Section 5 and Figure 2 of the paper.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - Likert-scale responses were used to measure perceived correctness, security, and trust. The scale ranged from "Strongly Disagree" to "Strongly Agree".
  - Trust in AI was specifically measured by the question: "I trusted the AI to produce secure code."
- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - Convert Likert-scale responses to numerical values for analysis (e.g., Strongly Disagree = 1, Strongly Agree = 5).
- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Calculate average scores for each question across participants in both the experiment and control groups.
  - Compare average scores between groups to assess differences in perceived security and trust.
