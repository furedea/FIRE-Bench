## Experimental Design

### 1. Large-scale Comparison of Process and Outcome Supervision
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the GPT-4 model as the base for both process-supervised reward models (PRMs) and outcome-supervised reward models (ORMs).
  - For PRMs, train using the PRM800K dataset, which contains step-level human feedback labels.
  - For ORMs, train using 100 uniformly sampled solutions per problem from the generator.
  - Evaluate both models on a representative subset of 500 problems from the MATH test set.
  - Use a best-of-N search strategy, where N varies, to select the highest-ranked solution by each reward model.
- **What to Record**: 
  - The percentage of problems solved correctly for each value of N.
  - Raw outputs of the models for each problem.
  - Intermediate statistics such as the number of correct steps identified by PRMs.
- **Correspondence**: Figure 3, Section 3.

### 2. Small-scale Synthetic Supervision Ablations
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use a large-scale PRM to supervise smaller models, simulating human feedback.
  - Sample between 1 and 200 solutions per problem from a small-scale generator.
  - Provide three forms of supervision: process supervision from the large PRM, outcome supervision from the large PRM, and outcome supervision from final-answer checking.
  - Evaluate the models by their best-of-500 selection and across different values of N.
- **What to Record**: 
  - The percentage of problems solved correctly for each supervision method and data collection scale.
  - Raw outputs and intermediate statistics for each supervision method.
- **Correspondence**: Figure 4, Section 4.1.

### 3. Active Learning Impact
- **Procedure**: 
  - Train a small-scale reward model (PRMselector) on a single sample per problem.
  - Use PRMselector to score 1000 samples per problem.
  - Select N samples per problem for training larger reward models, with 80% being the most convincing wrong-answer samples and 20% being the most convincing remaining samples.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**: 
  - Percentage of problems solved (Best-of-N): The fraction of problems for which the highest-ranked solution by the reward model is correct, evaluated over N solutions per problem.
- **Normalization/Pre-processing**: 
  - For PRMs, compute the probability that every step is correct and use the product of these probabilities as the solution score.
  - For ORMs, use the prediction at the final token as the overall score for the solution.
- **Aggregation Strategy**: 
  - Aggregate results per sample to determine the best solution per problem.
  - Aggregate per-condition results to compare the overall performance of PRMs and ORMs.

## Implementation Notes

- **Pitfalls/Caveats**: 
  - The training sets for PRMs and ORMs are not directly comparable due to differences in data collection strategies.
  - The automatic grading used for ORM targets is not perfectly reliable and may lead to misgraded solutions.
- **Special Prompt Templates**: 
  - Train the generator to produce solutions in a newline-delimited step-by-step format to facilitate step-level feedback.
- **Order of Operations**: 
  - Ensure that process supervision only provides feedback up to the first incorrect step to maintain a fair comparison with outcome supervision.
