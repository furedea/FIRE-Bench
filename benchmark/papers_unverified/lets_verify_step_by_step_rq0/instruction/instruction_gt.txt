## Experimental Design

### 1. Large-Scale Supervision Comparison
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the GPT-4 model as the base for both process-supervised reward models (PRMs) and outcome-supervised reward models (ORMs).
  - For PRMs, use the PRM800K dataset, which contains step-level human feedback labels. Train the PRM to predict the correctness of each step.
  - For ORMs, uniformly sample 100 solutions per problem from the generator and train the ORM to predict the correctness of the final solution.
  - Evaluate both models on a representative subset of 500 problems from the MATH test set.
  - Use a best-of-N search strategy, where N varies, to select the highest-ranked solution by the reward model.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Percentage of problems solved correctly by each model.
  - Performance comparison across different values of N.
- **Correspondence**: Figure 3, Section 3.

### 2. Small-Scale Synthetic Supervision
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use a large-scale PRM to supervise smaller models, simulating human feedback.
  - Sample between 1 and 200 solutions per problem from a small-scale generator.
  - Provide three forms of supervision: process supervision from PRMlarge, outcome supervision from PRMlarge, and outcome supervision from final-answer checking.
  - Evaluate the models by their best-of-500 selection.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Performance of each reward model across different data collection scales.
  - Best-of-N performance across different values of N.
- **Correspondence**: Figure 4, Section 4.

### 3. Active Learning Impact
- **Procedure**: 
  - Train a small-scale reward model, PRMselector, on a single sample per problem.
  - Use PRMselector to score 1000 samples per problem.
  - Select N samples per problem for training larger reward models, with 80% being the most convincing wrong-answer samples and 20% being the most convincing remaining samples.
  - Score the selected samples with PRMlarge and train on those scores.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - Percentage of problems solved: The fraction of problems for which the model's selected solution is correct.
  - Best-of-N performance: The percentage of problems solved when selecting the best solution out of N generated solutions.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - For PRMs, compute the probability that every step is correct and use the product of these probabilities as the solution score.
  - For ORMs, use the prediction at the final token as the overall score for the solution.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Aggregate performance by calculating the percentage of problems solved for each model and supervision type.
  - Compare performance across different values of N to assess the effectiveness of the reward models in selecting correct solutions.

## Implementation Notes

- **Prompt Templates and Output Parsing**:
  - Train the generator to produce solutions in a newline-delimited step-by-step format to facilitate step-level feedback.
  - Use specific thresholds for PRMlarge to determine step correctness, considering steps with a negative label probability greater than 20% as incorrect.

- **Order of Operations**:
  - Ensure that the PRM is trained to predict step correctness only up to the first incorrect step to maintain a fair comparison with ORM.
  - During active learning, iteratively retrain the PRM using the latest data to improve the selection of samples for human feedback.
