## Experimental Design

### 1. Tokenization Premiums for English-Centric Models
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the FLORES-200 dataset to evaluate tokenization lengths for English-centric models: GPT-2, RoBERTa, ChatGPT, GPT-4, and FlanT5.
  - For each model, tokenize the 2000 sentences in each of the 200 languages.
  - Calculate the tokenization premium for each language as the ratio of the tokenized length in that language to the tokenized length in English.
  - Ensure that languages with more than 10% of input characters mapped to UNK tokens are excluded from the analysis for FlanT5.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Raw tokenized lengths for each language.
  - Calculated tokenization premiums.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definition**: 
  - Tokenization Premium: For a sentence \( s_A \) in language A and its translation \( s_B \) in language B, the tokenization premium is defined as \( |t(s_A)|/|t(s_B)| \), where \( |t(s_A)| \) is the tokenized length of \( s_A \).
- **Normalization**: 
  - Ensure that tokenized lengths are normalized by dividing by the length of the English tokenization for each sentence.
- **Aggregation Strategy**: 
  - Calculate the average tokenization premium across all sentences for each language to obtain a per-language premium.
  - Compare these premiums across different models to assess disparities.

## Implementation Notes

- **Pitfalls**: 
  - Ensure that the tokenization process does not introduce additional tokens due to encoding errors or UNK tokens, especially for models like FlanT5.
- **Special Prompt Templates**: 
  - None specified, but ensure consistent tokenization settings across all models.
- **Order of Operations**: 
  - Tokenize each sentence in the dataset before calculating premiums to ensure consistency in length measurements.
