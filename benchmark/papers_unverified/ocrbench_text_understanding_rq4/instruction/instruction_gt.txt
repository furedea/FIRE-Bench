- **What to Record**:

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definition**: 
  - Use accuracy as the primary metric, defined as the percentage of correctly recognized expressions in LaTeX format compared to the ground truth.
  - Consider an expression correctly recognized if the entire LaTeX output matches the ground truth.
- **Pre-processing**: 
  - Normalize the model outputs by ensuring consistent LaTeX syntax and removing any extraneous explanatory text that may be generated by the models.
- **Aggregation Strategy**: 
  - Compute accuracy per sample.
  - Aggregate results to obtain an overall accuracy score for the entire test set.

- **Pitfalls and Caveats**: 
  - The paper notes that LMMs may struggle with the complex spatial structures of handwritten mathematical expressions and the indirect representation in LaTeX format.
  - Ensure that the models are not inadvertently fine-tuned on any part of the HME100K dataset to maintain the integrity of the zero-shot evaluation.
- **Prompt Template**: 
  - Use the specific prompt: "Please write out the expression of the formula in the image using LaTeX format." This prompt is crucial for guiding the models to output the expressions in the desired format.
- **Order of Operations**: 
  - First, load the test images from the HME100K dataset.
  - Apply the prompt to each image and record the model's output.
  - Post-process the output to extract the LaTeX expression.
  - Compare the extracted expression with the ground truth to compute accuracy.
