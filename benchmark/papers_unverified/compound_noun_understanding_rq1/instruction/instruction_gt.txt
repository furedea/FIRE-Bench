## Experimental Design

### 1. Retrieval with Example Captions
- **Procedure**: 
  - For each compound noun (CN) in the Compun benchmark, use GPT-4 to generate 5 diverse captions. The prompt to GPT-4 should be: 
    ```
    Return a list of 5 diverse captions with a compound_noun in a photo. The captions should be a maximum of 10 words and one-liners. All 5 captions should describe the compound noun in diverse settings with different verbs and actions being performed with the compound noun.
    ```
  - Construct 5 custom text prompts for each CN using the format: 
    ```
    A photo of a {class name}. An example of {compound name} in an image is {caption}.
    ```
  - Use these prompts to perform text-to-image retrieval with CLIP and OpenCLIP models.
  - Calculate the mean cosine similarity between each image and the 5 generated prompts.
  - Select the image with the highest mean similarity as the retrieval result.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Raw outputs of cosine similarity scores for each image-prompt pair.
  - The selected image for each CN based on the highest mean similarity.
  - Overall accuracy of retrieval for each model.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definition**: 
  - Use the evaluation metric \( f(C, P, N1, N2) \) defined as:
    \[
    f(C, P, N1, N2) =
    \begin{cases} 
    1 & \text{if } s(C, P) > s(C, N1) \text{ and } s(C, P) > s(C, N2) \\
    0 & \text{otherwise}
    \end{cases}
    \]
  - Here, \( s(.) \) is the cosine similarity between the text prompt and image embeddings.
- **Normalization and Pre-processing**: 
  - Ensure all text prompts are encoded using the CLIP text encoder.
  - Normalize image embeddings before computing cosine similarity.
- **Aggregation Strategy**: 
  - Compute mean similarity for each image across the 5 prompts.
  - Aggregate results to calculate overall accuracy by averaging the binary outcomes of \( f(C, P, N1, N2) \) across all CNs.
