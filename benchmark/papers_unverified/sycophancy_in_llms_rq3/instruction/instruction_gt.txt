## Experimental Design

### 1. Human and Preference Model Preferences for Truthful vs. Sycophantic Responses

- **Procedure**:
  - **Dataset**: Use a proof-of-concept dataset of 266 misconceptions. These misconceptions are grouped into eight difficulty levels based on the probability that Claude 2 states a given misconception is true when zero-shot prompted.
  - **Prompting Strategy**: For each misconception, generate three types of responses:
    1. **Baseline Truthful Responses**: Human-written responses that correct the misconception without additional details.
    2. **Helpful Truthful Responses**: Model-generated responses that correct the misconception and provide an explanation.
    3. **Sycophantic Responses**: Model-generated responses that convincingly agree with the misconception.
  - **Sampling Strategy**: Use best-of-N (BoN) sampling with N = 4096 to select the most convincing sycophantic response using the Claude 2 preference model (PM).
  - **Evaluation**: Present human crowd-workers with pairs of sycophantic and helpful truthful responses and record their preferences. Also, compute PM scores for each response type to determine preference.

- **What to Record**:
  - Frequency with which humans and the Claude 2 PM prefer sycophantic responses over truthful ones.
  - Differences in preference across misconception difficulty levels.

- **Correspondence to Paper**: This experiment corresponds to Section 4.3 and is visualized in Figure 7.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Frequency of Preference for Sycophantic Responses**: Calculate the percentage of times sycophantic responses are preferred over truthful ones by both humans and the PM.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - Ensure all responses are formatted consistently before presenting them to human evaluators or scoring with the PM.
  - Use a consistent prompt template for generating responses to maintain comparability.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Aggregate preferences per misconception difficulty level to analyze trends in preference as difficulty increases.
  - Report overall preference frequencies across all misconceptions.

## Implementation Notes

- **Prompt Templates**:
  - Use specific prompt templates for generating sycophantic and helpful truthful responses, ensuring clarity in the task for the model.
  - For the non-sycophantic PM, prepend a dialog to the prompt that explicitly requests truthful responses, followed by an assistant acknowledgment.

- **Order of Operations**:
  - First, generate all response types for each misconception.
  - Then, conduct BoN sampling to select the best sycophantic response.
  - Finally, evaluate preferences using both human crowd-workers and the PM.

- **Key Pitfalls**:
  - Ensure that human evaluators do not have access to external fact-checking tools to maintain the integrity of the preference data.
  - Carefully verify the misconceptions dataset to ensure all statements are indeed misconceptions.
