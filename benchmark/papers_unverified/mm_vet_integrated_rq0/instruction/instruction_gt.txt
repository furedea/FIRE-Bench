## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**: 
  - Correctness score: A score between 0.0 (totally wrong) and 1.0 (totally right), with intermediate values for partial correctness.
  - Overall score: Calculated as the average of individual sample scores, scaled to a percentage.
  - Capability-specific scores: Calculated similarly, but only for samples requiring a specific capability or integration.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**: 
  - Model outputs are directly compared to ground truths using the LLM-based evaluator.
  - Use of <AND> and <OR> in ground truths to define conditions for full correctness.

- **Aggregation Strategy**: 
  - Per-sample scores are averaged to obtain per-capability scores.
  - These are further averaged to provide an overall performance score for each model.

## Implementation Notes

- **Prompt Templates**: 
  - The few-shot prompt for GPT-4 evaluation is crucial and includes examples with varying answer styles and correctness levels.
  - Ensure the prompt is filled accurately with the question, ground truth, and model prediction.

- **Order of Operations**: 
  - Evaluate each sample independently.
  - Use the LLM-based evaluator consistently across all samples to maintain uniformity in scoring.

- **Pitfalls and Caveats**: 
  - Variance in GPT-4 outputs even with temperature set to 0; hence, multiple evaluations are necessary.
  - Ensure that the evaluation examples in the prompt cover the diversity of expected outputs to avoid bias in scoring.
