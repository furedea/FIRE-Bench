## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**: 
  - Correctness score is defined as a value between 0.0 (totally wrong) and 1.0 (totally right), with intermediate values for partial correctness.
  - Use the formula \( S = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^N s_i}{N} \times 100\% \) to compute the total score, where \( s_i \) is the score of sample \( i \) and \( N \) is the number of samples.
  - For capability-specific scores, use \( S_c = \frac{\sum s_i}{N_c} \times 100\% \), where \( C \) is the set of samples requiring a specific capability.
- **Normalization/Pre-processing**: 
  - Ensure that model outputs are formatted consistently with the ground truth for accurate scoring.
- **Aggregation Strategy**: 
  - Aggregate scores per sample, then per capability, and finally compute an overall score.
