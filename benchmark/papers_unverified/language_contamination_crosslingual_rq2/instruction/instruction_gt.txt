## Experimental Design

### 1. Language Composition Analysis
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the FastText language identification model to label each line in the pretraining corpora. 
  - Classify lines as non-English if they score above a confidence threshold of 0.6.
  - For C4.EN, subsample the first 50 million examples (14% of the dataset) for analysis.
  - Perform a manual qualitative analysis on a random subset of 200 non-English lines per corpus.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Estimated number of non-English tokens in each corpus.
  - Categories of non-English lines: NE (non-English), BiL (bilingual), Trans. (translations), Ent. (non-English entities), En. (misclassified English), XX (no natural language).
- **Correspondence**: Figure 1, Table 1, Section 2.1 and 2.2.

### 2. Cross-lingual Transfer Evaluation
- **Procedure**: 
  - Evaluate English pretrained models (BERT, RoBERTa, T5) on masked language modeling (MLM) and part-of-speech (POS) tagging tasks across 50+ languages.
  - Use Wiki-40B dataset for MLM evaluation, reporting bits per character (BPC).
  - For POS tagging, probe the final layer of each encoder with a linear classifier and fine-tune a subset of models.
- **What to Record**: 
  - MLM performance in BPC.
  - POS tagging accuracy for both probing and fine-tuned models.
- **Correspondence**: Figure 2, Tables 5, 6, 7, Section 3.1 and 3.2.

### 3. Correlation Analysis
- **Procedure**: 
  - Calculate Spearman correlation between task performance and two factors: the amount of target language data in pretraining and language similarity to English.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Correlation coefficients for each model and task.
- **Correspondence**: Table 2, Section 3.3.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Bits Per Character (BPC)**: Calculated for MLM tasks to compare models with different tokenizations.
  - **POS Tagging Accuracy**: Measured for both probing and fine-tuned models.
  - **Spearman Correlation**: Used to assess the relationship between task performance and potential transfer factors.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - For MLM, perform whole word masking on 15% of words in the Wiki-40B test set.
  - For POS tagging, average representations of subword tokens for words tokenized into multiple units.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - MLM and POS results are averaged across five runs with different random seeds.
  - Correlation analysis results are aggregated across tasks and models.

## Implementation Notes

- **Pitfalls and Caveats**:
  - Language detection is imperfect, especially for low-resource languages.
  - High UNK rates in tokenization can affect downstream task performance and MLM BPC scores.
  
- **Special Prompt Templates and Parsing**:
  - Ensure consistent tokenization across models for fair comparison.
  
- **Order of Operations**:
  - Perform language composition analysis before cross-lingual transfer evaluation to understand the dataset's multilingual nature.
