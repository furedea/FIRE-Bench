## Experimental Design

### 1. Evaluation of Textual Reasoning and Visual Awareness Capabilities
- **Procedure**:
  - **Textual Reasoning**: Provide models with text-only versions of each sample from the IQ50 dataset. These descriptions are written by a human expert and describe the puzzles without visual input.
  - **Visual Awareness**: Develop 20 questions designed to test the models on understanding shape, relative position, orientation, color, filling pattern, and fine-grained details. Use a subset of the IQ50 dataset for these questions.
  - **Prompting Strategy**: For textual reasoning, use zero-shot Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompts. For visual awareness, ask specific questions about the visual details of the images.
  - **Number of Samples**: Use the entire IQ50 dataset for textual reasoning and a subset for visual awareness.
  - **Shuffling/Ordering**: Randomly select samples for visual awareness questions to ensure a diverse set of visual features is tested.
- **What to Record**:
  - **Textual Reasoning**: Record the accuracy of the models in providing correct answers based on text-only descriptions.
  - **Visual Awareness**: Record the accuracy of the models in answering specific questions about visual details.
