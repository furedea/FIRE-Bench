## Experimental Design

### 1. Evaluation of Retrieval Performance with Perturbed Datasets

- **Procedure**:
  - **Input Format**: Use the COCO and Flickr30k datasets with perturbed captions and images.
  - **Perturbation Strategy**:
    - For captions, apply five types of perturbations: shuffle nouns and adjectives, shuffle everything but nouns and adjectives, shuffle trigrams, shuffle words within each trigram, and shuffle all words.
    - For images, shuffle rows, columns, and patches (9 equally-sized patches).
  - **Prompting Strategy**: Not applicable as this is a retrieval task.
  - **Number of Samples**: Use the full test splits of COCO (5k images) and Flickr30k (1k images).
  - **Shuffling/Ordering of Conditions**: Randomly shuffle the order of perturbations for each run to ensure robustness.
  - **Splits to Use**: Use the Karpathy test splits for both datasets.

- **What to Record**:
  - Record Recall@1 and Recall@5 for both text-to-image and image-to-text retrieval tasks.
  - Capture standard deviations across multiple runs (3 seeds) to account for randomness in perturbations.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions and Computation**:
  - **Recall@1**: The fraction of times the correct image (or text) is the top-ranked result.
  - **Recall@5**: The fraction of times the correct image (or text) is within the top 5 results.
  - Use standard library functions from evaluation frameworks to compute these metrics.

- **Normalization or Pre-processing**:
  - Ensure all text inputs are tokenized consistently using the same tokenizer as used during model training.
  - For image perturbations, ensure that the shuffling does not alter the image dimensions or introduce artifacts.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Compute per-sample metrics first.
  - Aggregate results to obtain per-condition averages.
  - Report overall averages and standard deviations across different seeds.

## Implementation Notes

- **Key Pitfalls or Caveats**:
  - Ensure that the perturbations do not introduce grammatical errors that could be trivially detected by the models.
  - Be cautious of any biases introduced by the random shuffling process; ensure multiple seeds are used to mitigate this.

- **Order of Operations**:
  - First, apply perturbations to the datasets.
  - Then, run the retrieval tasks using the perturbed datasets.
