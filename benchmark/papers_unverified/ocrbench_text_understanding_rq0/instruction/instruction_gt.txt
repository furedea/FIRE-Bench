## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Accuracy**: The percentage of correctly recognized text instances out of the total instances evaluated. This is calculated as:
    \[
    \text{Accuracy} = \frac{\text{Number of Correct Recognitions}}{\text{Total Number of Instances}} \times 100
    \]
  - For each dataset, compute the accuracy separately and then aggregate the results for overall performance comparison.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - Before scoring, ensure that the model outputs are stripped of any extraneous characters or explanations that do not pertain to the recognized text.
  - Convert all text to lowercase to ensure case-insensitivity unless specified otherwise by the dataset requirements.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Compute per-sample accuracy.
  - Aggregate per-dataset accuracy.
  - Finally, compute the overall accuracy across all datasets for each model.

## Implementation Notes

- **Prompt Templates**:
  - Use specific prompts for different types of text as mentioned in the procedure to ensure consistency in model evaluation.
  
- **Pitfalls and Caveats**:
  - The paper notes that LMMs may struggle with non-semantic text and handwritten text due to their reliance on semantic understanding. Ensure that these datasets are evaluated with particular attention to these limitations.
  
- **Order of Operations**:
