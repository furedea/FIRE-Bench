## Experimental Design

### 1. Evaluate LLM Performance on GSM-NoOp with Irrelevant Information
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the GSM-NoOp dataset, which includes mathematical problems with added irrelevant information.
  - Apply 8-shot Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting for each model.
  - For each problem, provide 8 examples (shots) that are variations of the same question, differing only in numerical values and irrelevant clauses.
  - Use greedy decoding for generating responses.
  - Ensure that the irrelevant information does not contribute to the reasoning chain needed to solve the problem.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Raw outputs of the models.
  - Extracted answers from the outputs.
  - Performance metrics such as accuracy and performance drop compared to GSM8K.
- **Correspondence**: 
  - This experiment corresponds to Section 4.4 and is illustrated in Figure 8 and Table 1 of the paper.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Accuracy**: The percentage of correctly solved problems out of the total number of problems.
  - **Performance Drop**: The difference in accuracy between the GSM8K dataset and the GSM-NoOp dataset, expressed as a percentage.
- **Normalization/Pre-processing**:
  - Ensure that model outputs are parsed to extract the final numerical answer.
  - Ignore any intermediate steps or irrelevant information in the output that do not contribute to the final answer.
- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Calculate accuracy per sample.
  - Aggregate accuracy across all samples to determine the overall performance for each model.
  - Compare the aggregated accuracy to the baseline performance on GSM8K to compute the performance drop.
