## Experimental Design

### 1. Agreement Evaluation between GPT-4 and Human Judges

- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the MT-bench dataset consisting of 80 high-quality multi-turn questions and the Chatbot Arena dataset with 30K crowdsourced votes.
  - For MT-bench, generate answers for all questions using six models: GPT-4, GPT-3.5, Claude-V1, Vicuna-13B, Alpaca-13B, and LLaMA-13B.
  - Use GPT-4 as an LLM judge to evaluate all pairs of model responses and compare these evaluations with those from 58 expert-level human labelers.
  - For Chatbot Arena, randomly sample 3K single-turn votes from the 30K dataset and compare GPT-4's evaluations with those from crowdsourced human judges.
  - Implement two types of LLM-as-a-judge evaluations: pairwise comparison and single-answer grading.

- **What to Record**: 
  - Record the agreement rate between GPT-4 and human judges for both MT-bench and Chatbot Arena datasets.
  - Record the number of votes and the percentage of agreement for each setup (including and excluding tie votes).

- **Correspondence to Paper**: 
  - This experiment corresponds to Section 4.2 and is detailed in Tables 5 and 6, as well as Figure 3 in the paper.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Agreement Rate**: Defined as the probability of randomly selected individuals (but not identical) of each type (GPT-4 or human) agreeing on a randomly selected question.
  - **Setup S1**: Includes non-tie, tie, and inconsistent votes (due to position bias) and counts inconsistent as tie.
  - **Setup S2**: Only includes non-tie votes.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - For single-answer grading, convert the scores into pairwise comparison results to calculate agreement.
  - Ensure that the order of responses does not influence the evaluation by using prompts that mitigate position bias.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Calculate the agreement rate for each setup (S1 and S2) separately.
  - Aggregate results across all questions and model pairs to determine overall agreement rates.

## Implementation Notes

- **Prompt Templates**:
  - Use the default prompt for pairwise comparison as shown in Figure 5 of the paper.
  - For single-answer grading, use the prompt in Figure 6.
  - Ensure prompts instruct the LLM judge to avoid position biases and not to favor certain names of the assistants.

- **Order of Operations**:
  - First, generate model responses for all questions.
  - Then, conduct LLM-as-a-judge evaluations using both pairwise comparison and single-answer grading.
  - Finally, compare LLM evaluations with human evaluations to calculate agreement rates.
