## Experimental Design

### 1. Red Teaming Task
- **Procedure**: 
  - Participants engage in open-ended conversations with an AI assistant to elicit harmful outputs.
  - Each conversation consists of multiple turns, with participants encouraged to focus on a single topic per attack.
  - Participants are presented with two model-generated responses per turn and select the more harmful one.
  - Conversations are typically 1-4 turns long, with some extending further.
  - Participants rate their success on a 5-point Likert scale (0 to 4).
  - Red team members are assigned to models randomly and are unaware of the model type.
- **Input Format**: 
  - Participants provide a short description of their intended attack strategy before starting.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Raw conversation transcripts.
  - Participant self-ratings of attack success.
  - Harmlessness scores for each AI response.
  - Task description harmlessness scores.

### 2. Review Task
- **Procedure**: 
  - A separate group of annotators reviews a sample of 500 attacks each on the 52B Prompted LM and RLHF models.
  - Annotators rate the success of attacks on the same 5-point Likert scale.
  - Annotators tag transcripts with up to 2 of 20 predefined topic tags.

## Evaluation Details

### Harmlessness Scoring
- **Metric Definition**: 
  - Harmlessness scores are computed using a preference model trained to predict less harmful responses.
  - Scores are aggregated using the minimum harmlessness score across all turns in a conversation.
- **Normalization**: 
  - Scores are normalized such that lower scores indicate more harmful outputs.
- **Aggregation Strategy**: 
  - Per-sample scores are aggregated to compute mean and minimum harmlessness scores for each model type and size.

### Attack Success Rating
- **Metric Definition**: 
  - Self-reported success on a 5-point Likert scale, where 0 indicates no success and 4 indicates high success.
- **Aggregation Strategy**: 
  - Average success rates are computed for each model type and size.

## Implementation Notes

- **Prompt Templates**: 
  - Use 1-shot learning for plain LMs and 14-shot learning for prompted LMs.
  - Rejection sampling involves generating 16 samples and selecting the 2 least harmful.
- **Output Parsing**: 
  - Ensure that the preference model scores each AI response conditioned on preceding dialogue.
- **Order of Operations**: 
  - Red team members must first describe their attack strategy before engaging in dialogue.
  - Annotators must complete the review task after the red team task data collection is complete.
