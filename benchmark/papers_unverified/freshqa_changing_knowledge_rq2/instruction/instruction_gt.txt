## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Accuracy (RELAXED)**: A response is credited if the primary answer is correct, even if additional information is hallucinated or outdated, as long as it does not contradict the primary answer.
  - **Accuracy (STRICT)**: A response is credited only if all information is accurate and up-to-date, with no hallucinations allowed.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - Ensure that the primary answer is extracted correctly from the model's output.
  - For numerical answers, approximate numbers are not accepted unless explicitly included in the ground-truth answers.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Calculate per-sample accuracy for each question.
  - Aggregate results to obtain per-condition accuracy (e.g., fast-changing, slow-changing).
  - Compute overall accuracy across all conditions.

## Implementation Notes

- **Prompt Templates**:
  - Use a unified format for retrieved evidences, including source webpage, date, title, text snippet, and highlighted words.
  - Include few-shot demonstrations at the beginning of the prompt to guide the model's reasoning process.

- **Order of Operations**:
  - Retrieve evidences from the search engine and sort them chronologically before including them in the prompt.
  - Ensure that the prompt includes both the question and the retrieved evidences in the specified order.

- **Pitfalls and Caveats**:
  - Be cautious of the model's tendency to hallucinate when provided with verbose demonstrations.
  - Ensure that the model's responses are evaluated consistently under both RELAXED and STRICT criteria to maintain reliability.
