## Evaluation Details

### Position Bias Mitigation
- **Metric Definition**: Consistency is defined as the percentage of cases where the LLM judge gives the same result regardless of the order of answers. Bias towards the first or second position is measured as the percentage of cases where the LLM favors one position over the other.
- **Normalization**: Ensure that the input format is consistent across all trials to avoid introducing variability unrelated to position bias.
- **Aggregation Strategy**: Calculate the overall consistency and bias percentages across all evaluated pairs.

### Reference-Guided Grading for Math Questions
- **Metric Definition**: Failure rate is defined as the percentage of cases where the LLM judge incorrectly evaluates the math question. This is calculated by comparing the LLM's judgment against the reference solution.
- **Normalization**: Ensure that the reference solution is clearly presented and formatted consistently with the assistant answers.
- **Aggregation Strategy**: Aggregate failure rates across all math questions to determine the overall effectiveness of the reference-guided approach.

## Implementation Notes
