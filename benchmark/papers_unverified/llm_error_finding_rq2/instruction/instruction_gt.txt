## Experimental Design

### 1. Mistake Finding with LLMs
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use three different prompting methods: direct trace-level prompting, direct step-level prompting, and CoT step-level prompting.
  - For direct trace-level prompting, input the entire trace and prompt for the mistake location, expecting a step number or "No" for no mistakes.
  - For direct step-level prompting, input the partial trace up to each step and prompt for a binary Yes/No output indicating correctness.
  - For CoT step-level prompting, extend direct step-level prompting by asking the model to reason through each step before providing a Yes/No output.
  - Use 3-shot prompting for all methods.
  - Evaluate on the BIG-Bench Mistake dataset across five tasks: word sorting, tracking shuffled objects, logical deduction, multi-step arithmetic, and Dyck languages.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Mistake location accuracy for each method and task.
  - Separate accuracy for traces with and without mistakes.

### 2. Correction with Oracle Mistake Location
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use a backtracking method with oracle mistake location information.
  - For traces with mistakes, regenerate the step at the mistake location using temperature = 1 until a different step is generated (up to 8 attempts).
  - Replace the original step with the new one and regenerate the remaining steps at temperature = 0.
  - Compare the corrected trace's final answer to the original.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Changes in accuracy for traces originally correct and incorrect.
  - Compare against a random baseline where mistake locations are randomly selected.
- **Correspondence**: Table 6, Section 4.

### 3. Classifier-Based Mistake Location
- **Procedure**: 
  - Train a small classifier (PaLM 2 Otter) on out-of-domain tasks from the BIG-Bench Mistake dataset.
  - Evaluate the classifier's mistake-finding accuracy on a held-out task.
  - Use the classifier to simulate mistake location accuracy at various levels and assess the impact on backtracking effectiveness.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Mistake finding accuracy of the classifier compared to few-shot prompting.
  - Changes in downstream task accuracy with simulated classifier accuracy.
- **Correspondence**: Table 7, Figure 3, Section 5.

## Evaluation Details

- **Mistake Location Accuracy**: 
  - Defined as the percentage of traces where the model correctly identifies the first mistake step or correctly identifies that there are no mistakes.
  - Calculated separately for traces with and without mistakes.

- **Downstream Task Accuracy**: 
  - Measured as the percentage of traces where the final answer is correct after correction.
  - Changes in accuracy are reported as absolute differences before and after correction.

- **Classifier Training and Evaluation**: 
  - Fine-tune the classifier on four tasks and evaluate on the fifth.
  - Use mistake finding accuracy as the primary metric, comparing against few-shot prompting results.
