## Experimental Design

### 1. Multilingual Grade School Math (MGSM) Evaluation
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the MGSM dataset, which consists of 250 math problems translated into ten languages.
  - Evaluate using four prompting strategies:
    - **DIRECT**: Provide the problem in the native language and expect a direct answer.
    - **NATIVE-COT**: Provide the problem in the native language and expect a step-by-step solution in the same language.
    - **EN-COT**: Provide the problem in the native language but expect a step-by-step solution in English.
    - **TRANSLATE-EN**: Translate the problem to English and expect a step-by-step solution in English.
  - Use few-shot prompting with up to 6 exemplars, adjusting for token limits in GPT-3.
  - Conduct experiments using both GPT-3 and PaLM models.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Raw model outputs for each problem and prompting strategy.
  - Accuracy of the model's final answers compared to the correct answers.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Accuracy**: The percentage of problems for which the model's final answer matches the correct answer.
  - Compute accuracy separately for each language and aggregate across all languages for overall performance.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:

  - For EN-COT and TRANSLATE-EN, ensure that intermediate reasoning steps are correctly parsed and lead to the final answer.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Calculate per-sample accuracy.
  - Aggregate per-language accuracy.
  - Compute overall accuracy by averaging across all languages.
