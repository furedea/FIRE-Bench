## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Pearson Correlation**: Measures the linear correlation between G-EVAL scores and human judgments.
  - **Spearman Correlation**: Assesses the rank-order correlation between G-EVAL scores and human judgments.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - Use the probabilities of output tokens from the LLM to normalize scores.
  - Calculate the final score as a weighted sum of predefined scores, using the formula:
    \[
    \text{score} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} p(s_i) \times s_i
    \]
  - Here, \( p(s_i) \) is the probability of score \( s_i \) as calculated by the LLM.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Compute per-sample scores for each dialogue turn.
  - Aggregate scores per evaluation aspect (naturalness, coherence, engagingness, groundedness).
  - Calculate overall Pearson and Spearman correlations across all samples.

## Implementation Notes

- **Prompt Templates**:
  - Use specific prompt templates for each evaluation aspect, as detailed in the paper's appendix.
  - Ensure prompts include clear instructions and evaluation criteria.

- **Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Prompting**:
  - CoT prompting is crucial for guiding the model through detailed evaluation steps.
  - Ensure the CoT includes reading the conversation, understanding the context, and applying the evaluation criteria.

- **Order of Operations**:
  - First, generate the CoT using the LLM.
  - Then, evaluate the dialogue response using the generated CoT and form-filling paradigm.
  - Finally, apply probability normalization to obtain the final scores.
