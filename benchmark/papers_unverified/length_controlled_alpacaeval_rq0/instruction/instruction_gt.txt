## Experimental Design

### 1. Length Gameability Experiment
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the AlpacaEval dataset consisting of 805 instructions.
  - For each instruction, generate responses using the GPT-4 and Claude-2.1 models.
  - Prompt the models with two different verbosity instructions:
    - "Answer with as much detail as possible." (verbose)
    - "Be as concise as possible while still providing all the necessary information to answer the question." (concise)
  - Randomize the order of model and baseline presentation to control for order bias.
- **What to Record**:
  - Raw outputs from both models for each verbosity condition.
  - Win rates for each model under both verbosity conditions.
  - Normalized standard deviation of win rates across verbosity conditions.
- **Correspondence**: This experiment corresponds to Section 4.1 and Figure 3 in the paper.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Win Rate**: The expected probability that the auto-evaluator prefers the evaluated model's output over the baseline's output, expressed as a percentage.
  - **Normalized Standard Deviation**: Calculated as the standard deviation of win rates across different verbosity prompts, normalized by the mean win rate to assess gameability.
  
- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - Standardize the length of outputs by subtracting the mean and dividing by the standard deviation of length differences.
  - Apply a tanh transformation to the standardized length differences to account for diminishing returns on log odds.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Compute win rates for each verbosity condition per model.
  - Aggregate results by calculating the normalized standard deviation across verbosity conditions to assess gameability.
