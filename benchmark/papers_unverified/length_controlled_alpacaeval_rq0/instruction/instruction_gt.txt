## Experimental Design

### 1. Length-Controlled AlpacaEval

- **Procedure**:
  - Collect a dataset of ratings `{x, zm, zb, m, b, y}` from the AlpacaEval leaderboard.
  - Fit a Generalized Linear Model (GLM) to predict the preference `y` using the features: model identity, length of output, and instruction difficulty.
  - Use logistic regression with the formula:
    \[
    q_{\theta,\phi,\psi}(y = 1|zm, zb, m, b, x) = \text{logistic}\left(\theta_m - \theta_b + \phi_{m,b} \cdot \tanh\left(\frac{\text{len}(zm) - \text{len}(zb)}{\text{std}(\text{len}(zm) - \text{len}(zb))}\right) + (\psi_m - \psi_b)\gamma_x\right)
    \]
  - Zero out the length term to obtain counterfactual estimates of AlpacaEval win rate:
    \[
    \text{winrate}_{LC}(m, b) = 100 \cdot \mathbb{E}_x\left[\text{logistic}\left(\theta_m - \theta_b + (\psi_m - \psi_b)\gamma_x\right)\right]
    \]
  - Use 5-fold cross-validation with L2 regularization to fit the model parameters.

- **What to Record**:
  - Raw outputs of the GLM predictions.
  - Length-corrected win rates.
  - Spearman correlation with Chatbot Arena.
  - Normalized standard deviation of win rates across verbosity prompts.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Spearman Correlation**: Measure the rank correlation between AlpacaEval-LC and Chatbot Arena rankings.
  - **Length Gameability**: Compute the normalized standard deviation of win rates across different verbosity prompts (concise, standard, verbose).

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - Standardize the length difference by dividing by its standard deviation.
  - Apply a `tanh` transformation to the standardized length difference to account for diminishing returns.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Compute per-sample predictions using the GLM.
  - Aggregate predictions to obtain per-condition win rates.
  - Calculate overall metrics such as Spearman correlation and gameability.
