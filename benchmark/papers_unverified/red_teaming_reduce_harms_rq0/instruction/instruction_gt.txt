## Experimental Design

### 1. Red Team Attack Success Evaluation
- **Procedure**: 
  - Conduct red teaming on models of sizes 2.7B, 13B, and 52B parameters across four model types: plain language model (LM), prompted LM, rejection sampling (RS) model, and reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) model.
  - Use a custom-built interface for red team members to engage in open-ended conversations with the AI assistant, aiming to elicit harmful outputs.
  - Each red team member is assigned to a model at random and is unaware of the model type they are interacting with.
  - Red team members are instructed to focus on a single topic per attack and to avoid obvious profanity.
  - Each conversation consists of multiple turns, typically 1-4, with the red team member providing a short description of their attack strategy before starting.
  - After each turn, present the red team member with two possible model-generated responses and ask them to select the more harmful one.
  - Collect self-reported success ratings from red team members on a 5-point Likert scale (0 to 4) after each conversation.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Raw conversation transcripts.
  - Self-reported attack success ratings.
  - Harmlessness scores for each model response, calculated using a preference model.
  - Minimum harmlessness score for each conversation.
- **Correspondence**: Figure 1, Table 1, Section 3.1

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Mean Attack Success**: Average of self-reported success ratings across all red team attempts for each model size and type.
  - **Mean Minimum Harmlessness Score**: Average of the minimum harmlessness scores for each conversation, where a lower score indicates more harmful outputs.
- **Computation**:
  - Use a preference model to compute harmlessness scores for each model response. The preference model is trained to predict which responses are less harmful based on red team data.
  - Aggregate scores by computing the mean and minimum harmlessness scores for each conversation.
- **Normalization**:
  - Normalize self-reported success ratings by converting them to a scale from 0 to 1 for consistency in analysis.
- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Compute per-sample harmlessness scores for each model response.
  - Aggregate per-sample scores to obtain per-condition (model type and size) statistics.
  - Report overall trends and differences across model types and sizes.
