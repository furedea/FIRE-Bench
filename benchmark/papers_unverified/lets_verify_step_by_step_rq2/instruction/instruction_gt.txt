## Experimental Design

### 1. Large-Scale Supervision with PRM800K
- **Procedure**: 
  - Train a large-scale Process-Supervised Reward Model (PRM) using the PRM800K dataset.
  - Evaluate the PRM by performing a best-of-N search over uniformly sampled solutions from the generator.
  - For each test problem, select the solution ranked highest by the PRM and automatically grade it based on its final answer.
  - Use a subset of the MATH test set for evaluation, specifically the 500 held-out problems.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Record the percentage of problems solved correctly (best-of-N performance).
  - Compare the performance of PRM against Outcome-Supervised Reward Models (ORM) and majority voting.
- **Correspondence**: Figure 3, Section 3

### 2. Small-Scale Synthetic Supervision
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the large-scale PRM to supervise smaller models by providing both process and outcome supervision.
  - Sample between 1 and 200 solutions per problem from a small-scale generator.
  - Provide three forms of supervision: process supervision from PRMlarge, outcome supervision from PRMlarge, and outcome supervision from final-answer checking.
  - Evaluate each reward model by its best-of-500 selection.
- **What to Record**: 
  - Record the best-of-N performance across different values of N.
  - Compare the effectiveness of process supervision versus outcome supervision.
- **Correspondence**: Figure 4a, Figure 4b, Section 4.1

### 3. Active Learning Impact
- **Procedure**: 
  - Train a small-scale reward model, PRMselector, on a single sample from each problem.
  - Use PRMselector to score 1000 samples per problem.
  - Select N samples per problem such that 80% are the most convincing wrong-answer samples, and 20% are the most convincing samples that remain.
  - Score the selected samples with PRMlarge and train on those scores.
- **What to Record**:

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Best-of-N Performance**: The fraction of problems solved correctly when selecting the best solution out of N generated solutions.
  - **PRM Score for a Solution**: The probability that every step is correct under the PRM, implemented as the product of the correctness probabilities for each step.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - For PRM scoring, consider neutral steps as positive and use the product of step-level scores as the reduction strategy.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Aggregate performance by calculating the percentage of problems solved correctly for each condition (e.g., PRM, ORM, majority voting) and compare across different values of N.

## Implementation Notes

- **Prompt Templates and Output Parsing**:
  - Train the generator to produce solutions in a newline-delimited step-by-step format to facilitate parsing individual steps.

- **Order of Operations**:
  - Ensure that the PRM is trained to predict the correctness of each step after the last token in each step, and perform a single PRM forward pass over the whole solution to determine step-level predictions.

- **Active Learning Strategy**:
  - Iteratively retrain the PRM using the latest data at several points in the data collection process, selecting the most convincing wrong-answer solutions for labeling.
