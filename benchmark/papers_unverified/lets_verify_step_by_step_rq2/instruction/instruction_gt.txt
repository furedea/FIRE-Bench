- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the large-scale PRM trained on the PRM800K dataset to evaluate its performance on a subset of the MATH test set.
  - Generate 1860 solutions per problem using the generator model.
  - Evaluate the PRM's ability to select the correct solution using a best-of-N search strategy.
  - Compare the performance of the PRM against the ORM and a majority voting baseline.

### 2. Small-Scale Synthetic Supervision Experiment
- **Procedure**: 
  - Use the large-scale PRM to supervise the training of smaller reward models.
  - Sample between 1 and 200 solutions per problem from a small-scale generator.
  - Provide three forms of supervision: process supervision from PRMlarge, outcome supervision from PRMlarge, and outcome supervision from final-answer checking.
  - Evaluate the best reward model from each series by its best-of-N performance across different values of N.
- **What to Record**:

  - Best-of-N performance across different values of N.
- **Correspondence**: Figure 4a and 4b, Section 4.1.

### 3. Active Learning Experiment
- **Procedure**: 
  - Train a small-scale reward model, PRMselector, on a single sample from each problem.
  - Use PRMselector to score 1000 samples per problem.
  - Select N samples per problem for training larger reward models, with 80% being the most convincing wrong-answer samples and 20% being the most convincing remaining samples.

## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **% Problems Solved (Best-of-N)**: The percentage of problems for which the highest-ranked solution (out of N generated solutions) is correct.
  - **Scoring Strategy for PRM**: Use the product of step-level scores, considering neutral steps as positive, to compute a solution-level score.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - For PRM scoring, compute the probability that every step is correct and use the product of these probabilities as the solution score.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Aggregate performance metrics by computing the percentage of problems solved for each condition (e.g., different values of N) and compare across different supervision methods.

## Implementation Notes

- **Pitfalls and Caveats**:
  - Ensure that the threshold for determining step correctness in synthetic supervision is set appropriately (e.g., a step is incorrect if PRMlarge assigns a negative label with >20% probability).
  - Be cautious of potential biases introduced by the active learning strategy, which favors labeling high-scoring wrong-answer solutions.

- **Special Prompt Templates**:
  - Train the generator to produce solutions in a newline-delimited step-by-step format to facilitate step-level evaluation.

- **Order of Operations**:
  - For active learning, iteratively retrain PRMselector using the latest data before selecting samples for the next iteration of data collection.
