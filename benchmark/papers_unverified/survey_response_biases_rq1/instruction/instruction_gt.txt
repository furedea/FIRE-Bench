## Evaluation Details

- **Metric Definitions**:
  - **Change in Response Distribution (\(\Delta p\))**: Calculated as the difference in counts of specific response options between the original and perturbed questions.
  - **Statistical Significance**: Use a Student’s t-test to determine if the average change (\(\bar{\Delta p}\)) is significantly different from 0, with a p-value threshold of 0.05.

- **Normalization and Pre-processing**:
  - Ensure that all responses are valid (i.e., match one of the provided options).
  - Normalize response distributions by the total number of responses collected per question.

- **Aggregation Strategy**:
  - Compute \(\Delta p\) for each question pair.
  - Aggregate \(\Delta p\) across all questions to compute \(\bar{\Delta p}\).
  - Conduct statistical tests to evaluate significance across the dataset.

## Implementation Notes

- **Prompt Template**:
  - Use a consistent prompt template for all models: "Please answer the following question with one of the alphabetical options provided. Question: [question] A. [option] B. [option] ... E. [option] Answer:"
  - Ensure models generate only one token for the response.

- **Pitfalls**:
  - Ensure that perturbations do not render questions nonsensical, especially for numeric or punctuated content.
  - Validate that the models' responses are correctly parsed and mapped to the intended options.
